The Argument for Coherence
I

Ornamental Restraint

The same geometric border language carries from physical label to digital frame. Neither surface adds decoration beyond necessity — the ornament serves as punctuation, not noise.

II

Chromatic Unity

Warm gold on deep night. The palette admits no variation across channels. What is printed is what is lit. The eye learns the brand before the mind does.

III

Typographic Voice

A single typeface governs both surfaces. Its weight shifts from heavy to italic in the same pattern on label as on screen. The brand speaks with one voice, always.
